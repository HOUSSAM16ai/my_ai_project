# Contributing to the Youth AI Safety Toolkit

Thank you for your interest in contributing! We welcome help from educators, developers, researchers, and youth safety advocates.

## Ways to Contribute
- **Content:** Review lesson plans, suggest new scenarios, translate materials.
- **Code:** Improve the interactive web modules (see `/app`).
- **Research:** Help design evaluation instruments or analyze data.
- **Design:** Improve the UX/UI for youth participants.

## How to Propose Changes

1.  **Check Issues:** Look for open issues or open a new one to discuss your idea.
2.  **Fork & Branch:** Create a fork and use a descriptive branch name (e.g., `content/bias-module-update`).
3.  **Make Changes:** Keep changes small and focused.
4.  **Test:** Ensure any code changes work and content is fact-checked.
5.  **Submit PR:** Open a Pull Request referencing the issue.

## Review Process
- All PRs require review by at least one maintainer.
- **Content PRs:** Reviewed for accuracy, age-appropriateness, and alignment with `SAFEGUARDING.md`.
- **Code PRs:** Reviewed for security, performance, and accessibility.

## DCO (Developer Certificate of Origin)
By contributing, you certify that you have the right to submit your work under the open license (CC BY 4.0 or MIT/Apache) used by this project.
- Please sign off your commits (`git commit -s`).

# Stakeholder Outputs: Templates

> **Four actionable templates to translate youth audit findings into impact.**

## 1. Policymaker Brief (Template)

**Audience**: Regulators, Ministry of Education, Youth Policy Advisors.
**Purpose**: To highlight gaps in current AI safety standards impacting youth.

### Structure
-   **Executive Summary**: "Youth found X vulnerabilities in Y popular AI models." (2 sentences)
-   **Key Findings**:
    -   *Risk 1*: [e.g., Self-Harm Content] - Frequency: X%.
    -   *Risk 2*: [e.g., Political Bias] - Frequency: Y%.
-   **Youth Perspective**: "We felt unsafe when..." (Direct quote).
-   **Recommendations**:
    -   Mandate age-appropriate design codes.
    -   Update national digital literacy curriculum.
-   **Call to Action**: "Support independent auditing."

### How to Use
-   Fill in data from `docs/IMPACT_MEASUREMENT_PLAN.md`.
-   Keep under 2 pages. Use visuals (charts from toolkit).

## 2. NGO Implementation Guide (Template)

**Audience**: Program Managers at Youth NGOs.
**Purpose**: To help partners run their own AI Safety Audit workshop.

### Structure
-   **Prerequisites**:
    -   Computers/Tablets.
    -   Consent Forms (Link to `SAFEGUARDING.md`).
    -   2 Facilitators.
-   **Session Plan (90 Mins)**:
    -   0-15: Intro & Safety Brief.
    -   15-45: Guided Audit (Use Toolkit Module A).
    -   45-75: Free Exploration (Use Toolkit Module B).
    -   75-90: Debrief & Surveys.
-   **Troubleshooting**: "What if a student sees something upsetting?" (See `SAFEGUARDING.md`).

### How to Use
-   Print as a checklist for facilitators.

## 3. Educator Training Pack (Template)

**Audience**: Teachers (CS, Civics, PSHE).
**Purpose**: To integrate AI safety into classroom lessons.

### Structure
-   **Learning Objectives**:
    -   Define "Algorithmic Bias".
    -   Identify 3 safety risks in Generative AI.
    -   Critique an AI output.
-   **Activity 1: "Spot the Fake"**: Use Toolkit Image Module to identify AI artifacts.
-   **Activity 2: "Red Teaming"**: Students try to trick the AI into being rude (within safe bounds).
-   **Discussion Guide**: Questions to prompt critical thinking.
-   **Homework**: "Interview a parent about their AI fears."

### How to Use
-   Downloadable slide deck + teacher notes.

## 4. AI Product Team Feedback Memo (Template)

**Audience**: Trust & Safety Teams at AI Labs (OpenAI, Google, etc.).
**Purpose**: To provide technical, actionable feedback on model failure modes.

### Structure
-   **Model Version**: [e.g., GPT-4o, Llama 3].
-   **Date of Audit**: [YYYY-MM-DD].
-   **Vulnerability Report**:
    -   *Issue ID*: #123.
    -   *Prompt*: "Write a story about..."
    -   *Expected Behavior*: Refusal or Safe response.
    -   *Actual Behavior*: Harmful output (describe).
    -   *Severity*: High/Medium/Low.
-   **Youth Context**: Why this matters to a 14-year-old user.

### How to Use
-   Submit via official "Red Teaming" or Bug Bounty channels.
-   Keep tone technical and objective.

# Stakeholder Outputs Strategy

**Purpose:** To translate our technical and research findings into practical, actionable formats for key stakeholders.

## 1. Policymaker Brief
**Audience:** Government officials, Education Ministries, Regulators.
**Goal:** Influence AI safety policy in education.

**Template Structure (1-2 Pages):**
-   **Executive Summary:** Key recommendation in 3 bullets.
-   **The Challenge:** Data on youth AI risks (from our research).
-   **The Evidence:** Results from our pilot (Efficacy).
-   **Recommendations:** 3 specific policy actions (e.g., "Mandate AI literacy in curriculum").
-   **Further Reading:** Link to full report.

## 2. NGO Implementation Guide
**Audience:** Youth organizations, After-school programs.
**Goal:** Enable easy adoption of the toolkit.

**Template Structure (Interactive PDF/Wiki):**
-   **Quick Start:** "Run a session in 10 minutes."
-   **Facilitator Script:** Step-by-step dialogue guide.
-   **Resource List:** Needs (WiFi, Devices, Printouts).
-   **Troubleshooting:** Common questions from youth.
-   **Safeguarding:** Quick reference checklist.

## 3. Educator Training Pack
**Audience:** Teachers, Librarians.
**Goal:** Build confidence to teach AI safety.

**Template Structure (Slide Deck + Notes):**
-   **Module 1:** "What is Generative AI?" (Basics).
-   **Module 2:** "Risks & Ethics" (Safety).
-   **Module 3:** "Using the Toolkit" (Pedagogy).
-   **Assessment:** Rubric for student work.

## 4. Product Team Feedback Memo
**Audience:** AI Companies, EdTech Developers.
**Goal:** Improve safety features in commercial tools.

**Template Structure (Memo):**
-   **Issue Identified:** Specific friction or risk point found during testing.
-   **User Voice:** Quotes/Data from youth participants.
-   **Proposed Solution:** Design suggestion (e.g., "Add age gate here").
-   **Impact:** Why this matters for their trust/safety metrics.
